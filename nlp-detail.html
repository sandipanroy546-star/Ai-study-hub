Language AI

Natural Language Processing

Bridging the gap between human communication and computer understanding.

What is NLP?

  • Subfield of Artificial Intelligence
  • Enables machines to understand human language
  • Works with text and speech data
  • Combines linguistics, machine learning and deep learning

Core Components

NLU (Understanding)

Interprets meaning from text, identifies intent and context, and handles language ambiguity.

NLG (Generation)

Produces meaningful text, converts structured data into language, used in chatbots and reporting.

Text Preprocessing Techniques

Tokenization

Splits text into words/sentences as the first step in NLP pipelines.

Stop Words

Removing common words like "is", "the", "and" to reduce noise.

Stemming

Fast but less accurate reduction of words to root forms.

Lemmatization

Accurate conversion to dictionary base forms using grammar.

Text Representation & Math

Traditional Methods

  • Bag of Words: Statistical frequency vectors.
  • TF-IDF: Measures word importance vs. irrelevance.

Modern Embeddings

  • Word2Vec: Capture semantic meaning as dense vectors.
  • GloVe: Global vectors for word representation.

The Transformer Revolution

Transformer Architecture & Attention

Modern NLP is built on Transformers which use Attention Mechanisms to focus on important words in parallel, handling long-range dependencies far better than older RNNs.

Common NLP Tasks

Sentiment Analysis

Identifies emotional tone (Positive, Negative, Neutral) in reviews and social media.

NER & Recognition

Identifies names, locations, and organizations to extract structured info from text.

Chatbots & Agents

Enable conversational interfaces that generate human-like responses to queries.

Ecosystem & Impact

Libraries & Tools

NLTK, SpaCy, Hugging Face Transformers, Gensim.

Applications

Search engines, Virtual assistants (Siri/Alexa), Spam filtering, Machine translation.

The Challenges

Handling sarcasm, deep context understanding, multilingual processing, and language ambiguity.
