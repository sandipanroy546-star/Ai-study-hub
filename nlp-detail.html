Natural Language Processing

Bridging the gap between human communication and computer understanding through specialized linguistic algorithms.

1. Introduction

Natural Language Processing (NLP) is a branch of artificial intelligence that gives computers the ability to understand, interpret, and generate human language in a way that is both meaningful and useful.

  • Combines linguistics and computer science
  • Handles both text and speech data
  • Essential for human-computer interaction

2. Key Concepts

Tokenization

Splitting text into individual units like words or subwords to be processed by models.

Word Embeddings

Representing words as high-dimensional vectors where similar meanings are close together (e.g., Word2Vec).

Transformers

Modern architectures using attention mechanisms to process language context in parallel.

3. Real-World Examples

ChatGPT & LLMs

Large Language Models use transformers to generate human-like text, code, and creative content based on prompts.

Google Translate

Neural Machine Translation models convert meaning from one language to another while preserving context and grammar.

Siri & Alexa

Intent recognition and entity extraction allow virtual assistants to understand and act upon spoken commands.

4. Industry Applications

Customer Support

Automated chatbots handling queries and routing tickets using sentiment analysis.

Legal & Compliance

Scanning thousands of documents to extract legal clauses and identify risks automatically.

Content Moderation

Identifying hate speech and harmful content on social media platforms in real-time.
