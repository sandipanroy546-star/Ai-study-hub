Visual AI

Computer Vision

Empowering machines with the ability to see, interpret, and understand the visual world.

What is Computer Vision?

  • Subfield of Artificial Intelligence
  • Enables machines to understand and interpret visual data
  • Works with images and videos
  • Mimics human visual perception

Image Representation & Data

Digital Image Structure

Composed of pixels arranged in rows and columns. Each pixel stores brightness information, and resolution directly affects clarity.

Matrix Representation

Images are represented as matrices. Color images typically use three channels (RGB) for red, green, and blue intensity values.

Preprocessing & Feature Extraction

Preprocessing

Resizing, normalization, and noise reduction to improve data quality for vision models.

Edge Detection

Identifying object boundaries by highlighting sudden intensity changes.

Segmentation

Dividing images into meaningful regions to separate objects from the background.

Extraction

Traditional methods like SIFT, SURF, and HOG used to identify important patterns.

The Power of CNNs

Convolutional Neural Networks

Specialized deep learning models that automatically extract hierarchical features using Convolution Layers (for edges and patterns) and Pooling Layers (to reduce dimensionality).

Primary Computer Vision Tasks

Object Detection

Identifying and locating multiple objects within an image. Essential for surveillance and self-driving cars.

Image Classification

Assigning a single label to an entire image (e.g., determining if a medical scan shows a tumor).

Face & Character Recognition

Biometric security (FaceRec) and converting images of text into editable data (OCR).

Ecosystem & Video Analysis

Video Analysis

Processing sequences of images to detect motion, behavior, and activity in real-time.

Libraries & Frameworks

OpenCV, TensorFlow, PyTorch, and Detectron.

Impact & Future Scope

Real-world Impact

Autonomous vehicles, medical imaging, industrial inspection, and advanced surveillance systems.

Future Vision

Next-gen real-time vision systems, revolutionary robotics, and high-adoption AI-powered imaging across sectors.
