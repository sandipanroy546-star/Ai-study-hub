Visual AI

Computer Vision

Empowering machines with the ability to see, interpret, and understand the visual world like human beings.

1. Introduction

Computer Vision (CV) is a field of artificial intelligence (AI) that enables computers and systems to derive meaningful information from digital images, videos and other visual inputs.

  • Mimics human visual perception
  • Analyzes pixel data to find structures
  • Bridges the gap between pixels and concepts

2. Key Concepts

Image Convolution

Applying filters to images to extract features like edges, textures, and shapes.

Object Detection

Identifying and locating instances of objects (e.g., cars, people) within an image or video.

Segmentation

Assigning a label to every pixel in an image to precisely define object boundaries.

3. Real-World Examples

Facial ID

Smartphones use computer vision to map facial geometry and grant secure access in milliseconds.

Medical Diagnosis

AI models scan X-rays and MRIs to detect anomalies like fractures or tumors with high precision.

AR/VR

Augmented reality apps track the physical environment to overlay digital objects onto the real world.

4. Industry Applications

Autonomous Driving

Real-time detection of lanes, traffic signs, and obstacles for safe navigation.

Robotics

Enabling robots to "see" and interact with objects in warehouses and manufacturing.

Security

Automated surveillance and crowd monitoring for public safety and traffic management.
